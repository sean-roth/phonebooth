# Founding-customer call — DRAFT, in the drawer

STATUS: DRAFT (2026-07-20). Not live doctrine. `dm-call-script.md` v1.1 is
what gets said on the phone until the app v0 exit criteria pass
(`app-v0-scope.md`). Never mix the two offers on one call — mentioning a
cheaper tool while selling the $3,500 pilot kills both (the Osborne rule:
announcing the future product murders the current one).

## The offer
Founding customer: first module built WITH them in the tool at a founding
price (four figures, TBD), then the tool at a founding monthly rate (TBD,
locked for founders — beta pricing gets set later, on usage data). Their
feedback shapes the product. Ownership explicit: hosted for convenience,
downloadable always — their documents, their training.

## Opener (v0.1 — beats inherited from v1.1: category, threat,
people-before-paper, wedge/kicker, two-yes ask)

> "Morning, this is Sean — I build onboarding courses for manufacturers.
> Most of how your floor runs is in your people's heads, and it clocks out
> with them at shift change. I've built a tool that sits down with your
> best guys, asks the right questions, folds in whatever you've got written
> down, and turns it into the course you hand every new guy — the whole
> crew ends up using it anyway. I'm taking a handful of founding shops
> before it opens up: first module built with me, the tool after, at a
> price that won't exist later. Got three minutes for four quick
> questions?"

Open question for the field: the voice doctrine bans "AI" as a costume
word. Selling a tool, the word may be unavoidable — or "a tool that
interviews your best guys" may carry it without saying it. Test both; log
which survives contact.

## The four questions
Unchanged from v1.1 — narrative, trigger, content + casting, history. They
scope the first module either way, and Q3's who-barrel now names who sits
with the interview.

## Closes
- **Fit:** "Founding spot: your first module, built with you, plus [n]
  months of the tool at the founding rate — $[TBD]."
- **Not now:** "We open more seats at beta. Want on the list?" Every
  non-close becomes a waitlist ask. The tracker gains a `Waitlist` status;
  the 60-day re-dial column and the warm threads are the seed of that list
  already.
- **Not a fit:** honest no, as always. The people you tell no remember you
  and refer.

## Switch discipline
The offer changes once, on a date, when v0's exit criteria pass — not
gradually, not experimentally, not "just this one call." Until then this
file stays in the drawer.
