# Segment Call Scripts — Warehouses & Property Management

*SOPs Nobody Reads · companion to `cold-call-pitch.md` (which stays locked and governs structure) · June 2026 pivot*

**What this is:** the noun swaps and segment-specific handlers for the two new segments. The opener's architecture, the 15-minute ask, the honest no-clients answer, the what-not-to-say list, the mechanical notes — all of that lives in `cold-call-pitch.md` and is unchanged. This doc only changes who you're talking to and what they push back with.

**One correction, codified (June 2026):** the qualifier — *"How often are you bringing on new people?"* — is a **mid-call tool, never the opener's closing line.** The opener ends on the 15-minute ask. Period. The qualifier comes out after he's engaged, and in these segments it gets you a number instead of "we aren't." Log the number in the tracker.

---

## THE OPENERS (same two beats, new noun)

**Warehouse / distributor:**

> Morning, this is Sean. I build custom onboarding for warehouses and distributors. I take your procedures and turn them into modules a new hire works through on their own. Worth fifteen minutes to see if it's a fit?

**Property management:**

> Morning, this is Sean. I build custom onboarding for property management companies. I take your procedures and turn them into modules a new maintenance tech works through on his own. Worth fifteen minutes to see if it's a fit?

Note the PM version says **"maintenance tech,"** not "new hire." It tells her in beat one that you know where her bleeding is. Don't say "employees" — leasing agents aren't the pain, techs are.

## GETTING PAST THE FRONT DESK (new — these segments have offices)

Trade shops had owners answering their own phones. These segments put a person between you and the decision. The play is the honest one — no tricks, no "he's expecting my call":

> Hi — I'm trying to reach whoever runs operations [PM: whoever runs maintenance]. Who would that be?

Then, always:

> And what's your name? — Thanks, [name].

**Write her name in the Notes column.** The Mary/STS thread exists because a gatekeeper became an ally. She is not an obstacle; she is the first sale. If she asks what it's about:

> I build onboarding modules from a company's own procedures. I'm hoping for fifteen minutes with [role] to see if it's a fit — what's the best way to get on his calendar?

Gatekeeper gets the **website**, never the demo link. Same rule as before: you're getting *past* her job function, not closing her.

## SEGMENT QUALIFIERS (mid-call, after interest)

- Both: *"How often are you bringing on new people?"* → expect a number. Log it.
- Warehouse, if the number is vague: *"Do you staff up for peak season?"* → plants the deadline (see triggers below).
- PM: *"How many techs do you run?"* → 1 tech per ~80–120 units is the industry norm; under 2 techs total, he may be too small. Over ~10, ask who owns training before pitching — corporate may.

## IF THEY PUSH BACK — WAREHOUSE

**"We use a staffing agency / mostly temps":**
> That's actually where this hits hardest. Temps churn the fastest and show up knowing nothing about your floor. The module gets every one of them safe and productive on day one — the agency doesn't do that part. You do.

**"We're not hiring right now":**
> Fair — though in this business the next hire is usually a when, not an if. And the same modules work for cross-training the crew you've got. Anything written down that guys still get wrong?

**"Corporate handles training" (branch of a big 3PL):**
Likely disqualifier. One probe, then a respectful exit:
> Got it — is there anything local that corporate's materials don't cover? ... No problem. Who at corporate would own something like this, just so I know who I'd be talking to?

**"What's it cost?"** — unchanged from the locked doc: pilot, $3,500, one topic, see if it works, go from there.

## IF THEY PUSH BACK — PROPERTY MANAGEMENT

**"We already have online training" (they mean a generic library — never name the competitor):**
> Those are libraries about everybody's buildings. I build training from your procedures — your boilers, your make-ready checklist, your work-order system. The new tech learns your buildings, not stock footage.

**"Our senior tech trains the new guys":**
> And he should — for the hands-on part. But right now he's your most expensive guy off the floor for two weeks every time someone starts. The module carries the repeatable eighty percent so he only has to teach the twenty that actually needs him.

**"We outsource our maintenance":**
Partial disqualifier. Probe once:
> Even the turns? ... [If fully outsourced:] Then I'm probably not a fit for you — appreciate the straight answer.

## THE TRIGGER PLANTS (use one, late in the call or in discovery)

- **Warehouse — the peak clock:** *"If we start now, the module's live before your peak crew walks in."* (June–July close → delivered before Aug–Oct ramp.)
- **PM — the public scoreboard:** *"What happens to your reviews when a tech quits mid-month?"* Slow repairs go public now; she knows it. Don't lecture — ask the question and let her say it.

## VOICEMAIL (same doctrine: a warmer redial, not a callback play)

> Hi, this is Sean — I build custom onboarding for [warehouses and distributors / property management companies] here in Chicago. Just wanted to reach whoever handles [operations / maintenance] — I'll try you again tomorrow.

Two VMs max per number, then rotate the lead to the 60-day lane.

## CALL WINDOWS (new — these segments keep different hours)

- **Food distribution:** docks open 4–5am; the owner is usually in by 8–9 and the morning rush is over by then. **Dial 9:00–11:00.** Friday afternoons are dead.
- **General wholesale (O'Hare corridor):** standard hours. 9:30–11:30.
- **PM offices:** 9–5. **Dial 9:30–11:30** — after the morning work-order triage, before lunch. Never Monday before 10 (weekend emergency backlog).

## WHAT NOT TO SAY (additions to the locked list)

- "OSHA" on call one with warehouses — same reason as "compliance." His CSP's lane, not yours. If *he* says lockout/tagout, the demo conversation just opened itself.
- "Tenants" when you mean her problem. Her problem is techs and turns. Tenants are her customer, not yours.
