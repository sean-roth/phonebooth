# After the Yes — Pilot Delivery Runbook

*SOPs Nobody Reads · companion to `cold-call-pitch.md` and `discovery-questions.md`*

**What this is:** the path from "yeah, I'm interested" to a delivered module and money in the account. If a contractor says yes and your stomach drops, open this. There is no improvising past the yes — every step is written down.

**The shape:** one call, one email, one kickoff. The sale closes on the phone. Everything after is paperwork and building. Seven steps, roughly nine days start to delivered.

---

## 0. The cold call — the only ask is 15 minutes

You are not selling the pilot here. You are asking for fifteen minutes. That is the whole ask.

- On a yes: *"Great — what's the best email for the calendar invite?"* Log it in the tracker (Email column). Book within 48 hours while it's warm.
- Text-confirm the morning of the booked call, from your Mint (312) number.

## 1. The 15-minute call — THIS is the sale

Run your four discovery questions (`discovery-questions.md`). Listen more than you talk. Question 3 is load-bearing — capture his answer **verbatim**. That sentence is the seed of the module and the spine of the build.

Close the call one of two ways, out loud:

> "Here's what I think would work for you, and here's what it costs — a [topic] module built from your procedures, $3,500, delivered within a week."

> "Honestly, I don't think I'm the right fit for what you need right now. Here's why." *(A respectful no builds credibility and referrals. Both endings are wins.)*

The proof move, when he's interested but hesitating: *"Want to see one first? I built a full Lockout/Tagout module from a real contractor's procedures — takes thirty minutes, you can do it from your phone."* → send the demo: **sopsnobodyreads.com/demo**

## 2. Same-day email — within the hour

This single email is your contract **and** your invoice. Speed is the game; every day between "sounds good" and money moving, the job site pulls him back under.

> **Subject:** Your [topic] training module — recap + next step
>
> [Name] — good talking just now. Quick recap so we're on the same page:
>
> **What I'm building:** a [topic] module from your procedures — [one line of scope from the call]. Self-paced. [SCORM for your LMS / hosted, see step 6].
> **Price:** $3,500, one-time. Delivered within a week of getting your materials.
> **To start:** [payment link]. Once that's in, I'll send a short list of what materials to send me.
>
> That's the whole thing. Reply if anything's off — otherwise I'll watch for the payment and we're moving.
>
> — Sean

## 3. Payment — $3,500, full, upfront

Full upfront, not a deposit. The amount is low, it's an explicit proof-of-concept, and the demo already proved you can build — so the usual "let me pay half and see" risk is mostly answered. Full upfront also halves your admin and kills the second-invoice chase.

- **Collection method — YOUR DECISION:**
  - *Card link (e.g. Stripe):* instant, frictionless for him, ~3% (~$105 on a pilot). **Recommended for the warm moment** — friction here costs you deals worth far more than the fee.
  - *Mercury ACH invoice:* no fee, but slower and a couple more steps on his end. **Confirm Mercury invoicing is still available in-app before relying on it.**
- Save 50/50 deposits for the $14k+ tiers where they actually matter.

## 4. Kickoff — keep his lift tiny

He's in the field. If the ask is heavy, he pays and then goes dark. Make it almost nothing.

> Hi [Name] — Sean here. Payment landed, thanks. Whenever you get a sec: send me whatever you've got on [topic] — a binder, a PDF, photos of the wall, even a voice memo walking me through how you actually do it. Messy is completely fine, I'll shape it. Once I have it I'll set up a quick 20-minute call to fill any gaps. The sooner I get it, the sooner you get the module.

His Q3 answer is already the seed — this just fills in the rest. Ask his LMS here too (drives the step-6 decision).

## 5. Build — 2–3 days real, one week promised

Your pipeline → the module. The one-week promise is buffer against image-model trouble. If you finish in three days, deliver on day five and look fast. Underpromise, overdeliver.

## 6. Deliver — and read the signal

- **Default: SCORM** package for their LMS. One-and-done.
- **Or: hosted** on a simple site you stand up, plus **$200–400/mo** hosting & maintenance. This is the tier-2 seed — a hosted single topic is the doorway to *"what's the next topic?"*
- The choice tells you who he is: SCORM = one-pilot buyer; hosting = future-program buyer. Note it and follow up accordingly.

---

**The honest read:** there is no void. From "yes" to "delivered" is one call, one email, one kickoff, and a build you have done before. The fear was real because the map didn't exist. Now it does.
