# Follow-Up Email Templates

*Companions to the after-the-yes runbook and the maybes layer. Plain text, short, no marketing voice — these read like a person, because they are one. Send from sean@ / contact@ per context. Demo link: sopsnobodyreads.com/demo.*

---

## 1. Post-conversation demo send (same day or next business morning)

**When:** any live conversation where interest surfaced but no discovery call was booked — especially "I'll pass it along" or "I'll see if anyone's interested." The email gives the circulator something concrete to circulate.

**Subject:** The training module I mentioned — [Company]

> Hi [Name] — thanks for taking my call this morning.
>
> You mentioned you'd see if anyone's interested, so here's something concrete to pass along: a sample training module built from an OSHA lockout/tagout procedure. Runs on a phone, about thirty minutes: sopsnobodyreads.com/demo
>
> One built for [Company] would come from your own procedures instead.
>
> [Callback line — see notes]
>
> Sean Roth
> SOPs Nobody Reads · sopsnobodyreads.com

**Notes:** The callback line matches whatever clock the call set — e.g. "I'll check back in January once things have settled" for a six-month callback; "I'll give you a ring Thursday like we said" for a scheduled one. Never add urgency the call didn't have. Send next business morning ~8–9am their time; a weekend send buries it.

## 2. Scheduled-callback confirmation (same day)

**When:** a prospect named a specific callback time.

**Subject:** Talk [day] — [Company]

> Hi [Name] — good talking today. Quick note to confirm I'll call [day, time, their zone].
>
> If you want a look before then, here's the sample module I mentioned — built from an OSHA lockout/tagout procedure: sopsnobodyreads.com/demo
>
> Sean

## 3. Long-clock re-warm (send ~1 week before a 60-day or 6-month callback fires)

**When:** the tracker's next-touch date is approaching on a long clock.

**Subject:** Checking back in — [Company]

> Hi [Name] — we spoke back in [month]; you mentioned [their reason, verbatim-ish: "new management was still settling in"]. I said I'd check back around now.
>
> Still doing the same thing: training modules built from a shop's own procedures. Sample here if useful: sopsnobodyreads.com/demo
>
> Worth fifteen minutes?
>
> Sean

---

**Hard rules:** one email per trigger, no drip sequences, no "just bumping this." The tracker owns the rhythm; email is a touch, not a campaign. Every send gets logged as a next-touch action, same as a dial.
