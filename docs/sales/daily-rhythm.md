# Daily Rhythm

What the dialing day looks like. The cadence matters more than the volume.

## The day

### 9:00–9:30 AM CT — Setup

- Coffee, water, food (don't dial hungry — your voice changes)
- Pull or refresh today's call list (15–20 numbers)
- Open Twilio dashboard
- Open script as a single-tab reference (don't read it — glance only)
- Stand up. You sound different standing.

### 9:30–11:30 AM CT — Dial block

- 18–22 dials
- Don't pause to overthink between calls
- After every 5 dials, 60-second stretch break
- Log each call as you go (don't batch at end — you'll forget)
- If a discovery call books, finish the dial block before sending the calendar invite

### 11:30–12:00 PM CT — Decompress

- Walk outside, ideally 15 minutes
- Don't check email or DMs
- Don't review calls yet — the nervous-system needs to reset first

### Afternoon — anything else

- Build/maintain phonebooth tool
- Discovery calls (schedule them for 2–4 PM CT slots — your voice is more relaxed by then)
- Module production for active clients
- The job application track (resume, portfolio, applications)

### 4:00–4:30 PM CT — Recording review

- Listen to 3 calls: best, worst, random
- Note one thing to adjust tomorrow
- Don't change the script wholesale — change one thing at a time

## Why 20 dials, not 40

You could blitz 40 in a day. You'd burn out by Wednesday. The 20-call pace forces a daily rhythm where you can debrief, adjust, sleep, and try again. After two weeks at 20, ramp to 30 if your voice is automatic. Do not start at 30.

The pace is also emotionally protective. Twenty rejections feel survivable. Forty don't.

## The week

- **Monday**: Dial. Recordings often best on Mondays — energy is fresh.
- **Tuesday**: Dial. Highest pickup rates of the week (research-backed). Make this your hardest list.
- **Wednesday**: Dial. Mid-week slump risk — keep the block short and tight.
- **Thursday**: Dial. Discovery calls cluster here.
- **Friday**: Half-day dialing (10 calls), then list building for next week, recording review for the week, and admin.

No dialing on weekends. Trade owners don't pick up. You also need a real day off.

## The month

### Week 1
- Voice will sound bad. Accept this.
- Goal: complete 100 dials. That's it.
- Expect 0 closes. Maybe 1 booked discovery call.

### Week 2
- Voice steadies. Opener becomes automatic.
- Goal: 100 dials, 3–5 booked discovery calls.
- First discovery calls happen this week.
- Listen back to your week 1 recordings vs. week 2 — you'll hear the difference.

### Week 3
- This is the danger week. Around day 12–14 your brain will say "this isn't working." It IS working — the cycle is just longer than the dialing window.
- Goal: 100 dials, 5–7 booked discovery calls, first proposal/SOW out.

### Week 4
- First close possible (not guaranteed).
- Goal: 100 dials, 5–8 discovery calls.
- Start tracking the conversion math: dials → conversations → discovery → close.

### Month 2
- Conversion improves. Expect 1 close per ~50 dials by week 6–8.
- Two closes minimum by end of month 2 if you've maintained pace.
- Begin raising prices on new prospects after client #3.

## What success looks like

**Day 1:** You completed the dial block intact. You made 18+ calls. You logged every one. You didn't quit at call 5.

**Week 1:** You finished the week with 100 dials logged and you didn't change the script three times. Your voice is steadier on Friday than Monday.

**Week 2:** A discovery call happens. Maybe two. You feel the rhythm.

**Week 4:** First close possible. First case study in the works.

**Month 2:** $20k+ in invoices. Predictable pipeline.

## What failure looks like (and how to detect it)

- **Dialing fewer than 15 in the block.** That's not a bad day, that's a pattern. Check: are you avoiding a specific objection? Is the list bad? Did you not eat?
- **No discovery calls booked after 200 dials.** Something's wrong with the script or the targeting. Listen back to 10 calls in a row and look for the pattern.
- **Discovery calls booked but not closing after 8+ calls.** Issue is in the discovery call itself, not the cold call. Re-read discovery-call.md.
- **Burning out.** If you find yourself dreading 9:30 AM more each day, drop to 15 dials for a week, not zero. Zero is harder to come back from than 15.

## Emotional regulation

The dial count is the metric. Not closes, not discovery calls — dials. You control the dial count. You don't control whether someone picks up or how their day is going.

When a call goes badly:
- Don't ruminate. Next dial.
- Don't take notes that feed your negative self-talk. Log facts only.
- The bad calls aren't about you. They're about timing, fit, and the owner's mood.

When a call goes well:
- Don't celebrate prematurely. Booked ≠ closed.
- Don't change the script just because that one call worked. n=1.
- Send the follow-up materials within an hour. Then keep dialing.

## When to break rhythm

Three legitimate reasons to skip a day:
- You're sick (real sick, not anxious-sick)
- A discovery call requires real prep (more than 30 min of prep is a real prep day)
- A wildfire evac (Conifer reality)

Anxiety is not a reason. The fix for anxiety is the dial, not the avoidance. Twenty calls reduces tomorrow's anxiety more than a day off does.

## What to do when a close lands

- Take 30 minutes off. Walk. Eat something good.
- Tell one person who matters. Not everyone — one person.
- Don't celebrate online yet. The client hasn't been delivered yet.
- Keep dialing the same afternoon. The momentum is the prize, not the dopamine.
