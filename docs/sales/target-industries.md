# Target Industries

Who to call, in what order, and why.

## Geographic focus

**Cook County, Illinois (Chicago metro).** You have a 312 number and a planned move. Stay focused here for the first 60 days. Don't spray.

Within Cook County, prioritize ZIP codes with industrial or commercial-construction density: 60607 (West Loop), 60612 (Near West Side), 60622 (Wicker Park / Bucktown industrial corridor), 60616–60619 (South Side trades), 60707 (Elmwood Park), 60804 (Cicero), and the inner-ring north suburbs (Skokie, Niles, Lincolnwood). These have higher concentrations of small-to-mid trade contractors than the Loop or pure residential neighborhoods.

If you exhaust Cook County, expand to DuPage and Lake counties before going regional.

## Primary targets (Track A — 15 calls/day)

Less-tooled trades with commercial-bid ambition. The trigger event matters more than the trade itself, but these are where the trigger event hits hardest:

### Small electrical contractors

- 5–25 employees
- Doing or wanting to do commercial work
- LOTO is genuinely high-stakes (energized work) — easy pain to surface
- Multiple OSHA-required topics: LOTO, electrical safety, PPE, fall protection, ladder safety, arc flash, sometimes confined space
- Bundle math works: 6–8 modules × $3.5k each = $20–28k, fits the starter/full range cleanly

### Mechanical / HVAC contractors doing commercial

- Same logic as electrical — LOTO matters, fall protection matters, refrigerant handling adds Hazcom
- Avoid pure residential HVAC — they're well-tooled (ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro) and don't need OSHA prequal
- Look for shops doing rooftop units, commercial chillers, industrial process work

### Specialty trades on commercial sites

- Fireproofing
- Insulation contractors
- Sheet metal shops doing commercial duct
- Glazing / commercial window install
- Demolition
- Concrete cutting / coring
- Welding shops

These trades are constantly on commercial sites where GC prequalification is non-negotiable. They're also less likely to have any formal training program at all.

## Secondary targets (Track B — 5 calls/day, after 2 weeks)

Ready when Track A gets predictable.

### Small manufacturing (light industrial)

- 10–50 employees
- Machine shops, fabrication, packaging operations
- OSHA scrutiny is real and routine
- Hazcom, LOTO, PPE, machine guarding all required

### Smaller GCs (5–25 employees)

- They're getting prequal demands from their own GCs (yes, this stacks)
- Multi-trade environments
- Higher deal sizes ($30k+ likely)
- Defer until you have 2–3 case studies — GCs need to see proof

## Filter criteria (use Google Maps tool)

When pulling lists, prioritize businesses that meet at least 3 of these:

- 5–25 employees (estimated from "team" pages, LinkedIn, or review patterns)
- Real website (not a Wix template from 2018, but doesn't need to be polished)
- Visible commercial work in their portfolio (project photos, case studies, testimonials)
- Listed on commercial-bid platforms (Avetta, ISN, ComplyWorks) — sometimes mentioned on their site
- Reviews mention safety, compliance, certifications, or commercial clients
- Based in Cook County, owner-operator likely

**Skip:**
- 1-truck owner operators (no training need)
- 50+ employee shops (already have an LMS, different sale)
- Pure residential trades (no GC prequal pressure)
- Brand-new companies (<2 years) — usually not buying yet
- Anything with corporate parentage / franchise (wrong decision-maker)

## Trigger events to listen for

These are the buying triggers. Hearing any of these on a cold call means lean in:

1. **"We just lost a bid because we couldn't show training records."** — Highest-intent signal possible.
2. **"Our insurance just went up."** — EMR or premium pressure. Often tied to incident history.
3. **"OSHA was just here."** — Citation or visit. Open wallet.
4. **"We had a [near-miss / incident] last [time]."** — Acute pain.
5. **"We're trying to bid on [bigger commercial work / public contracts]."** — Anticipated trigger. Slower sale but real.
6. **"GC asked us for [Avetta / ISN / safety prequal docs]."** — Concrete artifact required.

If none of these surface in the cold call, the prospect is in your demo-pipeline list, not your active pipeline. Send the demo, log the contact, move on.

## What you're NOT calling

### Why not law firms

Legal tech is the most VC-funded AI vertical right now. Every solo lawyer has been pitched ten times. The structural headwind: selling AI to lawyers means selling them tools that eat their billable hours. Defer.

### Why not residential HVAC / plumbing

Well-tooled segment. They're using ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber. They get pitched constantly. You'd be the eleventh caller this month. Different sale, different price point.

### Why not pure retail (small boutiques, local shops)

Low margin (20–40% gross, single-digit net). Q4-funded — May is the worst time to ask for $5k+. No training trigger event for most of them. Tech-comfort variance is high.

Exception: high-end specialty retail (jewelry, custom framing, art galleries) — they have margin and brand investment. But they're not a SOPs Nobody Reads target. Save them for the AI consulting track later.

### Why not manufacturers above 50 employees

They have an LMS already. The sale is replacing a system instead of building one. 6-month sales cycle, RFP process, security review. Wrong size for now.

## How to build the list

Use the Google Maps MCP tool (`@cablate/mcp-google-map`) once it's installed. Sample queries:

- "electrical contractors Cook County Illinois"
- "commercial HVAC contractors Chicago"
- "fireproofing contractors Chicago"
- "sheet metal contractors Chicago commercial"
- "insulation contractors Cook County"
- "commercial demolition Chicago"

For each: pull 30–50 results, filter to the criteria above, end up with 20–25 callable prospects per session. One hour of list-building → one day of dialing.

## How to log

Every dial gets a row in the Twilio dashboard or a spreadsheet:
- Business name
- Contact name (if you got it)
- Phone
- ZIP
- Outcome (no answer / VM / gatekeeper / conversation / discovery booked / not fit / demo sent)
- Trigger event mentioned (if any)
- Follow-up date
- Notes (one line max)

The tracking is the discipline. Without it, you'll convince yourself you're calling more than you are.
