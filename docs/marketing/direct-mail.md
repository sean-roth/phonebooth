# Direct Mail

Recurring postcard program targeting small Cook County contractors. Substitute for paid online ads.

## Why this works for this audience

Trade owners don't see Facebook ads (or block them, or their kid does). Don't search for SOPs on Google. Don't read newsletters from companies they don't know. They open mail — especially physical pieces that don't look like junk.

The brand name does work passively. The near-sale that came in earlier was triggered by name resonance, not outreach prose. Direct mail puts the name in front of contractors without requiring them to read or respond to anything.

## The tier-1 test (start here)

Validates whether postcard pre-warming meaningfully changes call outcomes before scaling spend.

- 50 postcards, hand-addressed (the hand-addressing is part of the test)
- ~$60 total cost
- Recipients: 50 small Cook County contractors that were going to be cold-called anyway
- Mail Monday
- Call Wednesday with the warm-up advantage: *"Hi, this is Sean from SOPs Nobody Reads — you might've gotten a postcard from me Monday."*
- Tracks: pickup rate, conversation rate, discovery-call conversion vs. cold-list baseline

**What success looks like:** measurable lift in pickup or conversation rate vs. cold list. Doesn't have to be huge. 10-20% lift validates the mechanism.

**What failure looks like:** zero meaningful difference. In that case, the brand-pull thesis is weaker than the n=1 suggested, and direct mail doesn't earn recurring spend.

## The recurring program (after tier 1 validates)

- 200 postcards/month
- Same Cook County contractor list, refreshed quarterly
- ~$150-200/month at scale
- Compounding name recognition over 6 months
- Funded by initial client revenue, not pre-revenue runway

Response rate math: one-shot direct mail to small businesses runs 0.5-1%. Recurring touches to the same list compound to 3-5% by touch 3-4. The first postcard doesn't have to convert. It just has to be remembered.

## Design principles

- **Brand name big and clean.** SOPs Nobody Reads is the asset. The piece exists to display it.
- One sentence about what you do. Not a paragraph.
- URL prominent.
- Phone number prominent.
- No QR code. Trade owners don't scan.
- 4x6 or 5x7 — standard postcard size, USPS marketing mail rate eligible at 200+ pieces.
- White space. The piece should not look busy. Cluttered postcards read as junk mail and go in the trash.
- A specific image: a screenshot of a real (anonymized) training slide, or the LOTO demo screen. Concrete beats decorative.

## Production options

- **Tier 1 (50 pieces):** Print at home or at a local print shop. Hand-address. Hand-stamp. ~$60 total.
- **Tier 2-3 (200-500 pieces):** Use Vistaprint, GotPrint, or PsPrint. Print + USPS Every Door Direct Mail or self-mail. $0.85-1.20 per piece all-in.
- **Tier 4+ (1000+ pieces):** Direct-mail house with mailing list management. Not relevant for the foreseeable future.

## What to track

In phonebooth or a separate sheet:

- Send date for each batch
- Recipient list (so calls can reference the mail)
- Inbound calls/emails referencing the postcard
- Discovery calls booked from postcard recipients
- Discovery calls booked from non-postcard cold list (control)
- Cost per discovery call by source

Review monthly. The compounding effect won't show in week 1; it shows in month 3.

## What goes on the postcard — first version

Front:
- **SOPs Nobody Reads** (large, clean typography)
- Tagline: "Training your crews actually complete."

Back:
- One sentence: "We turn the procedures you already have into interactive training, with a verifiable record. About a week per topic."
- Sean Roth
- Phone (312)
- sopsnobodyreads.com

Iterate after the tier-1 test based on what works.

## Rejection criteria

Don't proceed past tier 1 if:
- The test shows no measurable warm-call lift
- Postcards trigger more "how did you get my address" reactions than positive recognition
- Cost per discovery call from mail exceeds 3x cost per discovery call from cold-only

Do proceed if even one of those conditions clears.
