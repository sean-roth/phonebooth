# Marketing

Operating plan for everything that generates pipeline that isn't direct conversion. The `/sales` folder covers active calling and discovery. This folder covers brand, partnerships, content, organizations, and direct mail.

## North star

Get target prospects into discovery calls. That's the only conversion event that matters at this stage. Every channel either generates discovery calls directly (cold calling) or makes future discovery calls warmer (everything else).

## The layers run in parallel

Earlier framing had these sequenced (calling → mail → organizations → content). That was wrong. Each layer activates on its own trigger and runs independently. They compound because they touch the same audience from different angles, not because one prerequisites the next.

The one exception is partnerships, which gates on first case study. Everything else can start week one.

## The layers

- **Cold calling** — see `/sales`. The active selling engine.
- **Direct mail** — see `direct-mail.md`. Recurring postcards into Cook County contractor list. Substitute for paid online ads.
- **Partnerships** — see `partnerships.md`. Insurance brokers, GC compliance coordinators, safety consultants. Highest leverage; gates on first case study for full activation, but cold-call outreach to brokers can begin in dead windows immediately.
- **Organizations** — see `organizations.md`. Trade associations, member directories, newsletter sponsorships.
- **Content** — see `content.md`. Adult learning angle, not safety. Captured from work product as it happens.

## Weekly time budget (once running)

- Cold calling + follow-up infrastructure: ~70%
- Partnership outreach (broker calls, relationship building): ~10%
- Direct mail prep/send/list management: ~5%
- Organization participation: ~5%
- Content production from work product: ~5%
- Strategy review (this doc, weekly): ~5%

This is post-warmup. Weeks 1-2 are 90%+ cold calling because that's the muscle being built. Other layers come online as the calling rhythm stabilizes.

## Activation triggers

- **Cold calling** — active now
- **Direct mail tier-1 test** (50 hand-addressed postcards) — active now, $60 budget
- **Direct mail recurring** (200/month) — trigger: tier-1 test shows measurable warm-call effect
- **Insurance broker cold outreach** — active now, in dead windows between contractor calls
- **Trade association membership** — trigger: identify one to join by end of week 2; join in week 3
- **Newsletter sponsorship** — trigger: $X/month of recurring client revenue (decide threshold after first close)
- **Partnership full activation** (with finder's fee structure, formal pitch deck) — trigger: first paying client + case study artifact
- **Content publication** — trigger: 30 days of accumulated work product to draw from

## Cadence rules

- Strategy review every Friday at 4 PM CT, 30 minutes max. Look at what each layer produced. Adjust one thing.
- Don't add new layers mid-week. New activations on Mondays.
- Don't kill a layer until it has 4 weeks of data. Direct mail especially — the compounding only shows after touch 3-4.
- The parallelism doesn't mean equal effort. 70% of the work is still cold calling. The other layers are protected from being neglected by being in the calendar, not by being given equal time.

## What this folder is not

This is the operating plan, not a marketing strategy doc. No buyer personas, no SWOT, no positioning matrix. The positioning is settled ("instructional designer who converts your procedures"). The market is settled (small Cook County trades). What's left is execution cadence — what we do, when, how often, and what triggers what.

If the strategy needs to change, that's a different document. Don't edit this one to change strategy; replace it.
