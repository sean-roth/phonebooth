# Organizations

Trade association membership and industry presence. Slow burn, compounding return.

## Why this matters

- **Member directories are warm contact lists.** Members assume members are legitimate.
- **Newsletter sponsorships put the brand in trade-owner inboxes** with implicit association endorsement.
- **Events let the brand do passive work** — booth signage, badge logos, sponsor lists. Trade owners see the name without you pitching.
- **Credibility transfer.** "Member of NECA Chicago" on the website does work.
- **Committee work is the highest-leverage move inside any association** — if you serve on a safety, education, or workforce development committee, you become known as a person rather than a vendor.

## Targets to research

Research in week 1, pick one to join in week 3.

### Trades-specific
- **NECA Chicago Chapter** (electrical contractors)
- **IECA** (Independent Electrical Contractors Association)
- **MCAA Chicago** (Mechanical Contractors Association)
- **PCA** (Painting Contractors Association)
- **Builders Association of Chicago** (general contractors and broad trades)

### Safety-adjacent
- **ASSP Chicagoland Chapter** (American Society of Safety Professionals) — not your target audience for selling, but excellent for partnership relationships with safety consultants and CSPs
- **CSIA** (Construction Safety Institute / Construction Industry Service Corporation, regional chapters)

### Cross-trade business associations
- **Chicago Federation of Labor** — union-side context
- **Illinois Construction Industry Committee**
- **Local chamber of commerce** in the contractor's neighborhood (less leverage, but cheap)

## Activation order

- **Week 1-2:** Research. Read each association's website, member benefits, dues, event calendar. Identify one to start with.
- **Week 3:** Join one. Pay dues. Get listed in directory.
- **Week 3-6:** Attend two events. Don't pitch. Listen, learn names, exchange cards. The goal is presence, not conversion.
- **Month 2-3:** Volunteer for committee work if there's an opening. Especially safety, education, or workforce development committees — these touch your wheelhouse.
- **Month 3+:** Newsletter sponsorship if revenue allows. Most association newsletters run $200-500/month for regular sponsorship slots.
- **Month 6+:** Consider second association. Don't add a second one before month 6.

## Selection criteria for the first association

When choosing between candidates, weight:

1. Member size (bigger directory = more warm contacts)
2. Active newsletter or member communications (sponsorship surface)
3. Event frequency (more chances to be present)
4. Committee structure (volunteer leverage)
5. Annual dues (lower is better for first one)
6. Geographic match to Cook County

NECA Chicago is probably the right starting target because electrical is the primary trade focus, but verify dues and member access before committing.

## Rules

- **Don't pitch at events.** First three events are listening only. After that, mention what you do when asked, but don't lead with it. The trade-owner network has long memory and someone who pitched too hard at their first event gets quietly avoided.
- **Volunteer for committee work.** This is the actual leverage. A non-vendor presence on a safety committee turns into 5-10 introductions to people who matter.
- **Show up consistently.** Sporadic attendance produces nothing. Two events in three months is better than ten events in two months and then disappearing.
- **Don't try to be a thought leader on safety.** You're not a CSP. Be the instructional designer in the room, which is usually a unique role.

## Newsletter sponsorship economics

When revenue allows ($X/month threshold to be set after first close):

- $200-500/month for regular sponsor slot in monthly newsletter
- Reaches 200-2,000 trade-owner inboxes depending on association size
- Brand impression cost: roughly $0.10-0.50 per inbox per month
- Comparable to high-quality direct mail per impression, with implicit endorsement that mail doesn't have

Don't sponsor before joining. Membership first, sponsorship second.

## What to track

- Events attended (date, association, contacts made)
- Members in directory who match target profile
- Inbound from association directory ("saw your name on the NECA list…")
- Discovery calls sourced from association connections
- ROI per association at 6-month mark; cull if it isn't producing
