# Content

Brand visibility through expertise content. Captured from work product, not produced separately. Activation gates on accumulated work to draw from — roughly 30 days of client work or significant portfolio production.

## What you can write authoritatively

- **Adult learning principles applied to trades.** Your domain. 8,500 ESL classes is more direct teaching experience than most credentialed instructional designers.
- **Why training fails.** Cognitive science of retention, transfer, and behavior change. The universal principles, not the specific safety content.
- **Instructional design teardowns.** Take a public-domain corporate training video or a generic OSHA module, analyze why it doesn't stick, show what you'd do instead.
- **Before/after of SOPs → training.** Your actual work. Take a redacted client SOP (with permission), show the original document, show your slide treatment, walk through panel-logic decisions.
- **The conversion layer thesis.** What it means to take procedures someone else wrote and make them learnable. This is the positioning, written long-form.

## What you cannot write authoritatively

- OSHA opinions. You're not a CSP.
- Safety best practices for any specific trade.
- Trade-specific procedural advice (how to do LOTO correctly, etc.).

**Stay in your lane: instructional design applied to trades, not safety expertise.** This is also the safer position legally — you're not creating safety content liability for yourself by publishing opinions you'd later have to defend.

## Channels

- **LinkedIn posts.** 1/week starting month 3. 200-400 words each. Mix of teardowns, principles, and before/afters.
- **Long-form on the site.** 1/month. 1,000-2,000 words. Lives on `sopsnobodyreads.com/articles` or similar. Designed to be SEO-discoverable for terms like *"why OSHA training fails"* and *"custom safety training small contractor."*
- **Video walkthroughs (eventually).** 5-10 minute videos showing a real before/after. Higher production cost, higher impact. Month 6+.
- **Eventually a newsletter.** When you have 50+ qualified contacts to send to. Not before.

## The capture-from-work principle

Don't write content separately from client work. Document the work and edit it into content. Specifically:

- **Screenshots of bad source material.** Every client SOP you receive has cringe moments — walls of text, mid-90s clipart, contradictions, missing steps. Capture (with permission, redacted appropriately).
- **Slide treatments.** When you make a panel-logic decision, write down why. "Used a closure transition here because the source material assumed knowledge the learner didn't have." That note becomes content.
- **Outcomes data.** Once clients are running modules, capture completion rates, time-to-complete, comprehension check scores. Aggregate, anonymize, publish trend findings.
- **Drafts of script-writing decisions.** When you reject a turn of phrase as too sales-bro, save the rejected version with the replacement. "Here's what most safety training sounds like; here's what we do instead."

Content produced this way is dramatically better than content written cold because it's grounded in real artifacts. It's also free — the work was happening anyway.

## Posting cadence (when activated)

- LinkedIn: Tuesday and Thursday mornings, 9-10 AM CT. Pre-write Sunday, schedule with Buffer or LinkedIn native.
- Long-form: First Monday of each month.
- No posting on weekends; trade owners and brokers don't read content on weekends.

## Content inventory targets (first 90 days post-activation)

- 12 LinkedIn posts (one per week)
- 3 long-form articles
- 1 before/after video (optional, month 3)

This is enough volume to test what resonates without dominating the work week. If a particular angle pulls disproportionately (e.g., teardowns get 5x the engagement of principles posts), shift the mix toward what works.

## What this is not

- Not a content marketing flywheel. The audience is too narrow for that. 
- Not lead generation in the SEO sense. The cold calls and direct mail produce leads. Content makes those leads warmer.
- Not a brand-building exercise in the abstract. Every piece should make the cold call easier or the broker meeting smoother.

## Activation triggers

- 30 days of accumulated client work to draw from — OR — a significant portfolio production session that creates equivalent raw material.
- A clear angle for the first 5 posts (write them before publishing any).
- Consistent calling rhythm — don't add a content schedule before the calling rhythm is stable, or content will become avoidance.

## Rejection criteria

Don't continue past month 3 of content if:
- Engagement is consistently below 5 reactions per post (signals audience mismatch)
- Content is taking more than 2 hours per piece (capture-from-work principle is being violated)
- Cold calls aren't getting easier despite content presence (content isn't doing the warming work)

Do continue if any of those clear.
